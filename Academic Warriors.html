BDO121 - Introduction to Industrial Psychology

This module covers the fundamentals of industrial psychology, focusing on human behavior in the workplace, psychological assessment, and organizational development.

Access BDO121 Notes

PTO101 - Politics and International Relations

This module examines organized political society at national and international levels, focusing on political concepts, state governance, and the impact of globalization. It addresses regime development, national sovereignty challenges, and transnational issues like the environment and human rights.

EKN120 - Economics

This module covers core economic principles, focusing on macroeconomic measurement in South Africa's private and public sectors. It includes topics like aggregate demand and supply, economic growth, unemployment, inflation, microeconomics of government, international trade, and South Africa's role in the global economy.

INL120 - Information Science

This module delves into information systems, data management, and the role of information in society, preparing students for data-driven decision-making.

INL140 - Information Science

A continuation of the INL120 module, this section focuses on advanced topics in information science, such as knowledge management and information architecture.

PAD 122 - Public Administration

Public Administration introduces the constitutional principles of public administration, focusing on the South African governmental structure. It covers the roles, responsibilities, and authority of the executive, legislative, and judicial branches, as well as the functioning of the three tiers of government.

ENG 120 - English

English introduces students to post-nineteenth-century literature through the study of various texts across genres, including poetry, drama, and prose, from Africa and around the world. With two discussion classes per week, the module aims to equip students with the background knowledge and analytical skills necessary for perceptively reading modern and contemporary literary works.

KRM 120 - Criminology

Criminology covers Penology, focusing on key participants in the criminal justice system, and Crime Prevention and Control, addressing crime trends, criminal behavior theories, and prevention strategies.

SLK 120 - Psychology

Psychology provides an introduction to the biological basis of human behavior, covering key concepts, terminology, and principles of biological psychology. It explores the interconnection of biological systems and subsystems, while also examining cognitive processes such as perception, memory, thinking, intelligence, and creativity.
